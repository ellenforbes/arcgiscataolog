Download & Format Information

DXF Files
All DXF files available for download are in GDA94 MGA Zone 55.
Shapefiles
All shapefiles are in WGS84.
Need a KML?
Download the shapefile, zip all its parts together, then use the free online converter to convert the zip to KML: mygeodata.cloud/converter/shp-to-kml →
Imagery
Imagery is not available for download. Thiess monthly images will be linked in the layer description. Contact Ellen if you need a full image ECW sent to you.
Government Datasets
Government datasets (or subsets thereof) are not available for local download. A link to the original government data source can be found in the More Details description of the relevant layer and the data can be downloaded in it's original form from there.

Maps vs. Layers Explainer

What is a Layer?

A layer is one piece of spatial information — a table (like you'd see in Excel) with extra columns containing spatial coordinates. Those coordinates are read by the map to display the table as a visible spatial layer.

What is a Map?

One map can contain lots of layers and is a way to view and interact with lots of spatial data — like the Pembroke Site Map.
